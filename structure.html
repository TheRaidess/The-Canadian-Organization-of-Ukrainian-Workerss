Organizational Structure

Supreme Governing Body

The General Meeting of members is the highest governing body of the organization and is held at least once a year.

Board of Directors

Day-to-day management is carried out by the Board of Directors, elected for a 2-year term:

  • Chairperson: Represents and manages the organization.
  • Vice-Chairperson: Assists the Chairperson and acts in their absence.
  • Managing Director: Oversees operations and strategic planning.

Committees

Special committees and working groups are created for tasks like legal support, cultural events, and employment assistance.
